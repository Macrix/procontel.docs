In terms of ProconTEL, a distributed application consists of two or more of physically and logically decoupled computer programs. The physical separation means that programs can be hosted by different system processes - even on different computers across the network. The logical separation means that programs being part of a distributed application are developed as independent code units - typically they have no references to each other. There is also another, important assumption about programs being part of a distributed application: in the most of the possible use scenarios programs communicate with each other.
